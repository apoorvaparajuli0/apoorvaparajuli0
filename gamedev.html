Rue

Rue[Acceptance Level] is another team project I worked on in my second level design class. The premise of this project was that you have a man whose husband dies and you go through the different stages of grief while on drugs. I designed my level with multiple physical "levels" in the Unreal Engine, but they all conveyed my part of the Acceptance stage adequately and in the psychedelic/abstract manner my group was going for. For this project I was the team's designated mechanics programmer as well as being assigned part 2 of the Acceptance level to design and create as I saw fit, I set up the AI, the in-game UI, the ziplines, the PC's abilities etc. Due to my greater level of experience this time around, I was able to actually convey a narrative and implement some quality of life improvements to the game such as multidirectional movement, and dynamic footstep sounds depending on the surface the player steps on etc.

Killer Karens!

Killer Karens [Storeroom Level] was and is the first video game level I ever developed by myself. It's functions include but are not limited to, Enemy AI, FPS gameplay w/ Iron Sights, Ammo Pickups, Health Pickups, Grenades and more and I developed all of these myself in the span of approximately four months during a Game Level Design course on my surface pro laptop. The overall project was a team effort, there was a Coffee Shop level, an Alleyway level, a Bathroom level, and a Storeroom level and I worked the storeroom level and all of the mechanics within it. The overall "narrative" was, you, a coffee shop worker are defendning yourself against "Killer Karens" who are constantly attacking and harassing you and will not stop until you defeat them all.
